Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why does the Weak Acid titration curve have an initial "buffer region" before the steep climb?

As strong base is added, it reacts with the weak acid ($HA$) to form its conjugate base ($A^-$). A solution containing both a weak acid and its conjugate base forms a buffer. Buffers resist drastic changes in pH, creating the characteristic flattened region on the curve.
Question 2

At the Half-Equivalence point, what is the unique relationship between $pH$ and $pK_a$?

At exactly half-equivalence, half of the initial weak acid has been converted to its conjugate base, meaning $[HA] = [A^-]$. Plugging this into the Henderson-Hasselbalch equation ($pH = pK_a + \log(1)$) yields $pH = pK_a$.
Question 3

Why is the Equivalence Point of a Weak Acid + Strong Base NOT at pH = 7.0?

At the equivalence point, all the weak acid ($HA$) has been neutralized into its conjugate base ($A^-$). However, this conjugate base undergoes hydrolysis with water ($A^- + H_2O \rightleftharpoons HA + OH^-$), generating excess hydroxide ions. Thus, the solution is basic ($pH > 7$).
Question 4

Why is Phenolphthalein a poor choice of indicator for a Strong Acid + Weak Base titration?

An indicator must change color precisely within the steep vertical portion of the pH curve to accurately mark the equivalence point. Strong Acid + Weak Base titrations have equivalence points in the acidic range ($pH < 7$). Phenolphthalein changes color between pH 8.2 and 10.0, so it would change far too late!

Welcome to the Acid-Base Titration Virtual Laboratory! Analyze the neutralization curves of different acidic solutions.

🎯 Core Controls

  • Analyte Type: Toggle between a Strong Acid (HCl) and a Weak Acid (Acetic Acid). Notice how the starting pH and the shape of the curve change!
  • Concentrations & Volumes: Adjust $M_A$, $V_A$, and $M_B$. These determine exactly how much titrant ($V_B$) is required to reach the equivalence point ($M_A V_A = M_B V_B$).
  • Open Stopcock: Click to begin dropping NaOH into the flask. The real-time graph will trace the pH progression.
  • Indicators: Select Phenolphthalein to watch the solution physically turn pink as it crosses pH 8.2.
