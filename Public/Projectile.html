Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

At what angle is maximum horizontal range achieved, and how does height affect it?

For $h = 0$, maximum range is at $\theta = 45^\circ$: $$R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$ For launch height $h > 0$, optimal launch angle drops below $45^\circ$ to balance gravity: $$\theta_{opt} = \csc^{-1} \left( \sqrt{2 \left( 1 + \frac{gh}{v_0^2} \right)} \right)$$
Question 2

Why is the trajectory of a projectile independent of its mass in a vacuum?

In a vacuum, gravity is the only acting force. Newton's 2nd Law states: $$\Sigma \vec{F} = m \vec{a} \implies m \vec{g} = m \vec{a}$$ Mass cancels linearly from both sides, leaving gravity as the sole acceleration determinant: $$\vec{a}_{net} = \vec{g}$$
Question 3

How does air resistance alter the symmetry of the physical trajectory curve?

Aerodynamic drag forces scale non-linearly with velocity vector: $$\vec{F}_d = -\frac{1}{2} C_d \rho A v \vec{v}$$ This adds velocity-dependent deceleration components to both axes: $$\frac{dv_x}{dt} = -k v_x \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$$ The trajectory loses symmetry, rendering a steep terminal descent.
Question 4

How is the terminal velocity of a falling projectile calculated?

Terminal velocity occurs when downward gravity perfectly matches upward drag force: $$F_g = F_d \implies mg = \frac{1}{2} C_d \rho A v_t^2$$ Solving for $v_t$ gives: $$v_t = \sqrt{\frac{2mg}{C_d \rho A}}$$
Question 5

What are the equations for time-to-apogee and maximum height in ideal conditions?

At apogee, vertical velocity is zero: $v_y(t) = 0$. $$t_{apex} = \frac{v_0 \sin\theta}{g}$$ Substituting $t_{apex}$ into position coordinates yields maximum height $y_{max}$: $$y_{max} = h + \frac{v_0^2 \sin^2\theta}{2g}$$
* Physics verified via sub-stepped trajectory integrations. Apparatus Calibration: g = 9.80665 m/s² | ρ = 1.204 kg/m³

📘 PROJECTILE MOTION - User Guide

Welcome to the Interactive Projectile Kinematics Laboratory! Explore the flight physics, aerodynamics, and vector visualization of falling objects.

🎯 Core Controls

  • Fire Cannon Button: Launches a projectile based on current parameter controls.
  • Clear Paths Button: Clears all rendered curves, dots, and telemetry indicators.
  • Parameter Syncing: Use either the range slider or manual numeric input box to adjust Launch Height ($h$), Launch Angle ($\theta$), and Initial Velocity ($v_0$) synchronously.
  • Enable Air Resistance: Add non-linear fluid drag modeled on dynamic parameters (Mass, Diameter, Drag Coefficient) and fluid density.

🔍 Canvas & Interactive Viewport

  • Camera Zoom & Pan: Zoom in/out via the toolbar buttons, or **click and drag the canvas background** to pan horizontally or vertically! Recenter easily with the reset camera button.
  • Tape Measure: Toggle the ruler, then drag the start (orange square) and end (red crosshairs) to measure exact physical distances and angles.
  • Inspect Tool: Probe specific telemetry details by dragging the inspector crosshairs near any PhET trajectory milestone.
