Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What happens to the spacing of the interference fringes if the distance between the two slits ($d$) is decreased?

The fringe width is defined as $\beta = \frac{D\lambda}{d}$. Since the slit spacing $d$ lies in the denominator: $$\beta \propto \frac{1}{d}$$ If $d$ is decreased, the fringe width $\beta$ increases. Hence, the fringes spread further apart and grow wider.
Question 2

If the entire apparatus is submerged in water ($n = 1.33$), how does the fringe width change?

The wavelength of light in water decreases according to refractive index $n$: $$\lambda' = \frac{\lambda}{n} = \frac{\lambda}{1.33}$$ Because fringe width $\beta = \frac{D\lambda}{d}$ is directly proportional to wavelength: $$\beta' = \frac{\beta}{n} \approx 0.75 \beta$$ Thus, the fringe width compresses closer together by approximately $25\%$.
Question 3

Under what path difference condition does the central bright fringe ($n = 0$) form?

At the central line ($y = 0$), the geometric path length from both secondary sources $S_1$ and $S_2$ to the observer is identical: $$\Delta L = r_2 - r_1 = 0$$ A path difference of exactly zero causes a phase difference of zero, leading to completely constructive superposition: $$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \Delta L = 0$$
Question 4

Which optical spectral transition will compress the interference fringes closer on Screen C?

To compress the fringe pattern, we must decrease the fringe spacing $\beta$. Since $\beta = \frac{D\lambda}{d}$ and both $D$ and $d$ are constant parameters: $$\beta \propto \lambda$$ Thus, transitioning from a longer wavelength source (like red light, $\lambda \approx 650\text{ nm}$) to a shorter wavelength source (like blue light, $\lambda \approx 450\text{ nm}$) reduces the fringe width.
Question 5

For a monochromatic source of $\lambda = 500\text{ nm}$ and $d = 100\ \mu\text{m}$, what is the path difference at the 1st dark fringe?

Destructive cancellation occurs at odd half-wavelength differences: $$\Delta L = \left(m + \frac{1}{2}\right)\lambda \quad (m = 0, 1, 2...)$$ The first dark fringe occurs at $m = 0$, giving: $$\Delta L = \frac{1}{2}\lambda = \frac{1}{2} \times 500\text{ nm} = 250\text{ nm}$$
