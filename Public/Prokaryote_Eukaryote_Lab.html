Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What is the primary defining difference between a Prokaryote and a Eukaryote?

The defining feature is the Nucleus. Eukaryotes (Eu = true, karyon = kernel/nucleus) trap their linear DNA inside a double-membrane lipid envelope. Prokaryotes (Pro = before) lack a nucleus; their circular DNA floats freely in a region called the nucleoid.
Question 2

Do all Eukaryotes lack a Cell Wall?

No. While Animal cells lack a cell wall, Plant cells have a rigid cell wall made of cellulose, and Fungi have walls made of chitin. Prokaryotes almost always have cell walls made of peptidoglycan.
Question 3

Why do antibiotics kill bacteria but not human cells?

Many antibiotics target structures unique to prokaryotes. For example, Penicillin targets the peptidoglycan cell wall (which human cells lack). Tetracycline targets the smaller 70S Ribosomes of bacteria, leaving the larger 80S ribosomes of human cells completely untouched.
