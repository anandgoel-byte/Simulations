Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why does the parabola open downwards when $a$ is negative?

In the equation $x^2 = 4ay$, since $x^2$ must always be positive (or zero), the sign of $y$ must match the sign of $a$. If $a$ is negative, $y$ must also be negative, restricting the curve to the lower half of the coordinate plane.
Question 2

What happens to the curve as $a$ gets larger?

As the focal length $a$ increases, the Focus moves further from the Vertex. Geometrically, this causes the parabola to become wider or "flatter." A small $a$ results in a very narrow, "sharp" parabola.
Question 3

What is the "Latus Rectum"?

The latus rectum is the line segment that passes through the Focus, parallel to the Directrix, with both endpoints on the parabola. Its length is exactly $|4a|$. It provides a quick measure of how wide the parabola opens.
