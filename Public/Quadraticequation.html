📘 ALGEBRA PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the Quadratic Equation Visualizer! Explore how coefficients manipulate the shape and position of a parabola.

🎯 Core Controls

  • Coefficient a: Controls the width and direction. Make it negative to watch the parabola flip upside down!
  • Coefficient b: Shifts the axis of symmetry. Notice how it moves the vertex diagonally.
  • Constant c: This is your y-intercept. It moves the entire parabola up and down the y-axis.
  • The Discriminant ($\Delta$): Watch the live telemetry. When $\Delta < 0$, the parabola detaches from the x-axis, meaning the roots are imaginary!

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Visualizing Coefficients: Understand how coefficient $a$ dictates the width and direction of the parabola, and how $b$ and $c$ position the vertex $(h, k)$ and the $y$-intercept.
  • Analyzing the Discriminant: Connect the sign of the discriminant ($\Delta$) with the nature of the roots (real & distinct, real & equal, or imaginary) and their positions relative to the $x$-axis.
  • Interactive Graphing: Gain intuition about quadratic behavior by dragging key points (vertex, roots, intercepts) to see how coefficients and mathematical properties update in real-time.
