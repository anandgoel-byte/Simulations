📘 ELECTROPHYSIOLOGY PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the Neurobiology Laboratory! Observe the electrochemical signals that allow neurons to communicate.

🎯 Core Controls

  • Electrical Stimulus: Set the injection voltage and press "Apply". If the stimulus is too weak (fails to reach -55mV), the signal dies out. If it is strong enough, a full Action Potential fires!
  • Oscilloscope Trace: Watch the right side of the canvas to see the live recording of the membrane voltage over time, mapping exactly to Depolarization, Repolarization, and Hyperpolarization.
  • Ion Channels: Watch the Blue (Na+) and Green (K+) channels open and close in the membrane. Notice the "ball and chain" mechanism that inactivates the Na+ channel at the peak of the voltage curve.
  • Neurotoxins: Apply TTX to physically plug the Na+ channels. Try firing a stimulus—nothing will happen! Apply TEA to block K+ channels and watch how the membrane struggles to return to resting state.
