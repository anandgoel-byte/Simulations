Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why does light intensity have no effect on stopping potential?

Classically, intensity corresponds to electric field energy density. But quantum model models stopping potential strictly based on individual photon energies: $$eV_{\text{stop}} = hf - \Phi \implies V_{\text{stop}} = \frac{h}{e}f - \frac{\Phi}{e}$$ Increasing intensity only raises the number of photons (increasing photocurrent) but leaves their individual energy unchanged.
Question 2

What is the physical significance of the slope of the KE vs Frequency graph?

Comparing Einstein's equation $K_{\text{max}} = hf - \Phi$ to the straight line formula $y = mx + c$: - Slope $m = h$ (Planck's Constant). - X-intercept corresponds to the threshold frequency $f_0 = \frac{\Phi}{h}$. - Y-intercept corresponds to the negative of the work function $-\Phi$.
Question 3

Why is there no time delay in emission even under extremely faint light?

Classically, electrons absorb energy continuously across wave fronts, requiring: $$t = \frac{\Phi}{I \times A} \approx 10^7 \text{ seconds (over 100 days)}$$ In quantum physics, the energy transfer is an instantaneous, point-like collision between one photon packet and one electron, occurring in $t \le 10^{-9}$ seconds.
Question 4

Why does the photocurrent saturate under positive voltages?

At positive accelerating potentials, the electric field is strong enough to attract $100\%$ of emitted photoelectrons to the collector plate: $$I_{\text{saturation}} = e \frac{dN_e}{dt}$$ Further increases in voltage cannot pull more electrons since all that are emitted are already swept into the ammeter loop.
Question 5

What determines the threshold wavelength ($\lambda_0$) of a metal?

The work function ($\Phi$) is the binding threshold of electrons. The minimum energy corresponds directly to: $$\lambda_0 = \frac{hc}{\Phi}$$ Platinum with a high work function ($\Phi \approx 5.64\text{ eV}$) has a low $\lambda_0 \approx 220\text{ nm}$ (requires UV), whereas Cesium ($\Phi \approx 2.14\text{ eV}$) has $\lambda_0 \approx 580\text{ nm}$ (visible light).

🔬 Guided Virtual Laboratory Tasks

Task 1

Determine Threshold Frequency of Sodium

1. Select Sodium ($Na$) as cathode (Work function $\Phi \approx 2.36\text{ eV}$).
2. Set plate voltage to $0.0\text{ V}$ and intensity to $50\%$.
3. Sweep wavelength slowly from $800\text{ nm}$ down to UV and record where Ammeter is non-zero. Verify that $f_0 = \frac{c}{\lambda_0} \approx 5.7 \times 10^{14}\text{ Hz}$.

Task 2

Measure Planck's Constant ($h$) via stopping potentials

1. Select Potassium ($K$, $\Phi \approx 2.29\text{ eV}$).
2. Set wavelength to $300\text{ nm}$, $400\text{ nm}$, $500\text{ nm}$ successively.
3. For each, adjust voltage negatively until photocurrent is exactly $0.00\ \mu\text{A}$ and record $V_{\text{stop}}$. Plot values to verify slope is $\frac{h}{e}$.
