Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why does reactivity increase as you move down Group 1 (from Li to Cs)?

Reactivity is tied to First Ionization Energy (how easily the atom loses its one valence electron). As you move down the group, atomic radius increases. The outer electron gets further from the positive nucleus and experiences more "shielding" from inner electrons, making it much easier to remove (lower IE).
Question 2

What causes the distinct colored flames (e.g., Lilac for Potassium) during the reaction?

The reaction produces Hydrogen gas ($H_2$) and immense heat. This exothermic heat ignites the Hydrogen gas. The intense thermal energy also excites the electrons in the vaporized metal cations, which emit characteristic photons (colors) as they fall back to their ground state.
Question 3

Why does Phenolphthalein turn the water pink?

Phenolphthalein is an acid-base indicator that is colorless in neutral/acidic solutions but turns bright pink/magenta in basic solutions (pH > 8.2). The reaction of alkali metals with water produces metal hydroxides (e.g., $NaOH$, $KOH$), which are strong bases, flooding the water with $OH^-$ ions.
Question 4

Why does Sodium visually melt into a perfect sphere before disappearing?

The exothermic reaction generates enough heat to surpass Sodium's relatively low melting point ($97.8^\circ C$). Once it becomes a liquid metal droplet, the high surface tension of the liquid metal pulls it into a perfect sphere as it continues to violently react and dash across the water's surface.
