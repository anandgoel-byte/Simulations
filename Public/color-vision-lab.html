Question 1

Why does a yellow filter appear bright yellow under white light, but completely black when monochromatic blue light shines through it?

An optical bandpass filter works via subtractive absorption. A yellow filter transmits red and green light while absorbing blue light: $$T_{\text{yellow}}(\lambda) \approx 0 \quad \text{for } \lambda \in [380\text{ nm}, 490\text{ nm}]$$ When monochromatic blue light ($\lambda_0 \approx 450\text{ nm}$) is incident: $$I_{\text{out}}(\lambda_0) = I_{\text{in}}(\lambda_0) \cdot T_{\text{yellow}}(\lambda_0) = 0$$ This results in zero transmitted intensity (complete absorption).
Question 2

How does overlapping independent Red and Green photon streams cause the human eye to perceive yellow?

Human color photoreception is governed by three cone photopigments ($S, M, L$) with sensitivities $S(\lambda), M(\lambda), L(\lambda)$. Overlapping red ($\lambda_R \approx 650\text{ nm}$) and green ($\lambda_G \approx 530\text{ nm}$) light excites both L-cones and M-cones. The neural response vector matches monochromatic yellow ($\lambda_Y \approx 580\text{ nm}$): $$\vec{R}_{\text{mix}} = I_R \begin{pmatrix} L(\lambda_R) \\ M(\lambda_R) \\ S(\lambda_R) \end{pmatrix} + I_G \begin{pmatrix} L(\lambda_G) \\ M(\lambda_G) \\ S(\lambda_G) \end{pmatrix} \approx \begin{pmatrix} L(\lambda_Y) \\ M(\lambda_Y) \\ S(\lambda_Y) \end{pmatrix}$$ creating an identical perception.
Question 3

Which specific color is primarily absorbed when white light passes through a Cyan filter?

White light is a uniform spectrum: $W = R + G + B$. A cyan filter transmits only green and blue wavelengths, meaning its subtraction action is: $$\text{Cyan}(W) = W - R = G + B$$ Thus, Red is the complementary color absorbed by the Cyan filter.
Question 4

Using the relation $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$, which visible color of light carries the highest per-photon energy?

According to the Planck-Einstein relation, the energy of a single photon is inversely proportional to its wavelength: $$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda} = \frac{1240\text{ eV}\cdot\text{nm}}{\lambda}$$ Evaluating at the visible boundaries: * Violet ($\lambda \approx 400\text{ nm}$): $E \approx 3.10\text{ eV}$ * Red ($\lambda \approx 700\text{ nm}$): $E \approx 1.77\text{ eV}$ Thus, violet photons carry nearly double the quantum energy of red photons.
Question 5

A person with deuteranopia (red-green color blindness) is missing which type of cone cell?

Deuteranopia is a genetic condition caused by the absence of functional M-cones (peak sensitivity $\lambda_{\text{max}} \approx 534\text{ nm}$). Without the M-cone channel, the visual cortex cannot compute the red-green opponent color difference: $$\text{Red-Green Opponent Channel} \propto L - M$$ rendering the observer unable to distinguish red and green hues.
