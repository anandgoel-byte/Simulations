Definition Summary

Null Vector

A vector whose magnitude is zero is called a null vector or zero vector. It is denoted by $\vec{0}$. It has no definite direction, or can be regarded as having any direction.

Key Conceptual Fact

The physical significance of a null vector includes describing position vectors of particles at the origin, or the velocity vector of a stationary body.
