📘 ALGEBRA PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the Linear Systems Simulator. We are visualizing how two equations interact simultaneously on a graph.

The Three Realities

  • Intersecting (1 Solution): The most common scenario. The lines have different slopes, so they MUST cross exactly once. The yellow dot marks the coordinate $(x,y)$ that perfectly satisfies both equations.
  • Parallel (0 Solutions): Try setting $a_1=1, b_1=2$ and $a_2=1, b_2=2$, but make $c_1 \neq c_2$. The lines run side-by-side forever. Because they never touch, there is no shared point.
  • Coincident (Infinite Solutions): Make both equations completely identical (e.g., $1x+1y=2$ and $2x+2y=4$). One line draws directly on top of the other. Every single point on the line is a solution!
