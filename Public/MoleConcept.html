Scaling Metrics

Just as we group everyday small items (like eggs or donuts) by the dozen (12), chemists bundle extremely microscopic particles by the mole ($6.022 \times 10^{23}$) to enable macroscopic physical scale operations.

Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

How is Avogadro's number ($N_A$) defined and how does it relate to Carbon-12?

Avogadro's number corresponds to the total atoms inside exactly $12\text{ grams}$ of pure Carbon-12 ($^{12}\text{C}$): $$N_A = 6.02214 \times 10^{23} \text{ mol}^{-1}$$ Mass spectrometry places single atom Carbon-12 at: $$m_C = 1.99265 \times 10^{-23} \text{ g}$$ $$\text{Count} = \frac{12\text{ g}}{m_C} = 6.022 \times 10^{23}$$
Question 2

What is the mathematical scale difference between Molar Mass ($g/mol$) and molecular mass ($amu$)?

By atomic scaling limits, $1\text{ amu} = \frac{1}{N_A}\text{ grams}$. Hence, for a molecule of mass $A\text{ amu}$: $$\text{Mass of } 1\text{ mole} = A\text{ amu} \times N_A = A\text{ g}$$ This yields identical numerical values but distinct physical scales (macroscopic vs microscopic).
Question 3

Why does one mole of any ideal gas occupy exactly $22.71 \text{ L}$ at STP ($10^5 \text{ Pa}$)?

Under Avogadro's hypothesis and Ideal Gas formulas: $$V_m = \frac{RT}{P} = \frac{(8.314)(273.15)}{10^5} \approx 22.71\text{ L/mol}$$ Because the equation includes no mass or composition parameters, the volume is identical for all ideal gases.
Question 4

Why is the mole unit critical in laboratory chemistry instead of atomic mass units?

Individual atoms are too small to isolate (mass of H $\approx 1.67 \times 10^{-24}\text{ g}$). Moles act as a mathematical bridge linking mass directly to stoichiometric ratios: $$\text{Moles } (n) = \frac{\text{Mass } (m)}{\text{Molar Mass } (M)}$$
Question 5

How do we calculate the total atoms in $9.0\text{ grams}$ of liquid water ($H_2O$)?

First, calculate the moles of $H_2O$ molecules: $$n = \frac{9.0\text{ g}}{18.0\text{ g/mol}} = 0.5\text{ moles}$$ Molecules count: $N = 0.5 \times N_A = 3.011 \times 10^{23}$. Since water holds 3 atoms per molecule, total atoms is: $$\text{Atoms} = 3 \times N = 9.033 \times 10^{23}$$
* Stoichiometric equations calibrated via standard IUPAC atomic weight charts. Avogadro Constant: N_A = 6.02214076 × 10^23 mol^-1
