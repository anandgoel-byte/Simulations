Question 1

When will a line and a plane fail to intersect?

They fail to intersect when the line is perfectly parallel to the plane. Algebraically, this causes the denominator of our $t$ equation ($av_x+bv_y+cv_z$) to become zero, meaning the line's direction vector $\vec{v}$ is perpendicular to the plane's normal vector $\vec{n}$.
Question 2

What happens if BOTH the numerator and denominator are zero?

This yields an indeterminate solution ($0/0$). Geometrically, this means the line is parallel to the plane AND its starting point $P_0$ sits directly on the plane surface. Consequently, the line lies completely inside the plane, resulting in infinite intersections.
Question 3

What does the normal vector $\langle a,b,c \rangle$ dictate?

The coefficients $\langle a,b,c \rangle$ represent the vector that stands perfectly perpendicular (normal) to the plane's surface. Altering these values adjusts the directional orientation and tilt of the plane in 3D space.
* Singularities (parallel lines where denominator = 0) are handled gracefully by capping infinite values in the graphics engine.
