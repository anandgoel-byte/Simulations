Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why does changing light intensity have no effect on the maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons?

Under quantum theory, intensity represents only the rate of photons striking the plate. Higher intensity means more photons per second, creating a larger current, but individual photon energy ($E=hf$) remains unchanged: $$K_{\text{max}} = hf - \Phi$$ Thus, the maximum kinetic energy of freed electrons is entirely independent of light intensity.
Question 2

What is the physical significance of the slope of a Stopping Potential ($V_s$) vs Frequency ($f$) graph?

Rearranging Einstein's equation $eV_s = hf - \Phi$ into a linear equation $y = mx + c$: $$V_s = \left(\frac{h}{e}\right)f - \frac{\Phi}{e}$$ Comparing this, the slope of the curve ($m$) directly equals $\frac{h}{e}$ (Planck's constant divided by the elementary charge).
Question 3

If Zinc has a threshold frequency in the UV spectrum, what happens when intense visible violet light shines on it?

Absolutely zero emission occurs, regardless of intensity or duration. Because visible light photons lack the threshold energy required to overcome Zinc's work function: $$\Phi = 4.30\text{ eV} > E_{\text{violet}} \approx 3.1\text{ eV}$$ Thus, the photons cannot dislodge any electrons. This is robust evidence of the particle-like nature of light.
Question 4

What is the definition of Work Function ($\Phi$), and how does it relate to Threshold Frequency ($f_0$)?

The work function ($\Phi$) is the minimum thermodynamic work needed to remove an electron from a solid targetemitter surface to a point immediately outside the solid: $$\Phi = hf_0$$ If incoming photon energy $hf$ is less than $\Phi$, emission is impossible.
Question 5

Why does photocurrent saturate at high positive voltages?

Once the positive accelerating voltage is strong enough to pull $100\%$ of emitted photoelectrons to the collector plate: $$I_{\text{saturation}} = e \frac{dN_e}{dt}$$ Further increases in voltage cannot increase current, as the rate of electron emission is purely governed by light intensity.
