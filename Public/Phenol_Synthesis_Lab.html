📘 PHENOL SYNTHESIS PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the Aromatic Alcohol (Phenol) Synthesis Simulator. Explore 4 industrial and laboratory methods for preparing phenol from different starting materials.

Reaction Mechanisms

  • Cumene Process: The most important industrial route. Cumene is oxidized to cumene hydroperoxide, which undergoes acid-catalyzed rearrangement to yield phenol and acetone as a co-product.
  • Dow Process: Chlorobenzene reacts with aqueous NaOH under extreme conditions (300°C, 200 atm) via nucleophilic aromatic substitution to form sodium phenoxide, which is then acidified.
  • Diazonium Salt Route: Aniline is diazotized with NaNO₂/HCl at 0–5°C. Warming the diazonium salt with water releases N₂ gas and produces phenol.
  • Sulphonic Acid Fusion: Benzene sulphonic acid is fused with molten NaOH at 300°C. The –SO₃⁻ group is displaced by OH⁻, yielding sodium phenoxide. Acidification gives free phenol.
