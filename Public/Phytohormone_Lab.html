Question 1

What causes Apical Dominance?

Apical dominance is maintained primarily by Auxin, which is synthesized in the apical (top) bud and flows downwards. This high concentration of Auxin suppresses the growth of lateral (axillary) buds. Removing the top of a plant removes the Auxin source, allowing Cytokinin from the roots to trigger lateral branching, making the plant bushy.
Question 2

Which hormone is responsible for the "foolish seedling" disease (Bakanae)?

Gibberellin (GA). Historically discovered in rice plants infected by the fungus Gibberella fujikuroi, excessive GA causes extreme internode elongation. The plants grow so tall and spindly that they fall over.
Question 3

How do "one bad apple spoil the bunch"?

Ripening fruits release Ethylene, which is a gas. Because it is airborne, it diffuses to neighboring fruits, binding to their receptors and triggering a positive feedback loop that causes them to rapidly ripen and eventually rot as well.
