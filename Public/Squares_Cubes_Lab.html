📘 USER GUIDE

Welcome to the Squares & Cubes Visualizer! Let's explore why we use the terms "Square" and "Cube" in mathematics.

⏱️ Estimated Time: 5–10 minutes

How to Explore

  • 1D Line ($n$): A simple row of blocks. If the base number is 4, you have exactly 4 blocks in a line.
  • 2D Square ($n^2$): When you multiply a number by itself ($4 \times 4$), you literally create a flat, 2D square shape. It requires 16 blocks.
  • 3D Cube ($n^3$): When you multiply it three times ($4 \times 4 \times 4$), you create a solid 3D box. Watch how rapidly the total number of blocks explodes (4 becomes 64 blocks!).
  • Square Root ($\sqrt{N}$): Finds the side length of a square containing $N$ total unit blocks. The side length (the "root") is highlighted in glowing cyan.
  • Cube Root ($\sqrt[3]{N}$): Finds the edge length of a cube containing $N$ total unit blocks. The edge length (the "root") is highlighted in glowing cyan.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the geometric representations of algebraic powers ($n^1$, $n^2$, $n^3$) and roots ($\sqrt{N}$, $\sqrt[3]{N}$).
  • Develop spatial reasoning by orbiting, zooming, and counting unit blocks in 3D.
  • Connect algebraic formulas to volumetric grid scaling.
