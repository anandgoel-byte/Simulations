📘 Acidity Comparator Protocols

Explore how resonance stabilization of the conjugate base dictates acid strength across a series of phenols and ethanol.

🎯 Core Controls

  • Molecule Buttons: Switch between Ethanol, Phenol, p-Cresol, p-Nitrophenol. Watch how pKₐ and resonance forms change.
  • Deprotonate: A hydroxide base attacks, abstracting H⁺. The O—H bond breaks heterolytically, leaving a negative charge on oxygen.
  • Resonance Cycling: After deprotonation, the phenoxide ion's resonance structures animate. Blue curved arrows show electron pair movement.
  • Electron Flow Arrows: Toggle the checkbox to show/hide mechanistic curved arrows.

🧪 Key Concepts

  • Phenoxide: 4 resonance forms → charge on O, ortho-C, ortho-C, para-C
  • p-Nitrophenoxide: 5 resonance forms → extra form places charge on nitro O
  • Ethoxide: 0 resonance forms → charge localized on O only → weakest acid
