Validate Wien's Displacement Law

Black Body Radiation Physics

An idealized physical body that absorbs all incident electromagnetic radiation is termed a **Black Body**. When a black body is at a uniform temperature, it emits thermal electromagnetic radiation known as Black Body Radiation.

1. Planck's Law of Radiation

To solve the classical ultraviolet catastrophe, Max Planck proposed that energy is exchanged in discrete packets called quanta ($E = hf$). The spectral radiance of a black body at absolute temperature $T$ is given by:

$$I(\lambda, T) = \frac{2hc^2}{\lambda^5} \frac{1}{e^{\frac{hc}{\lambda k_B T}} - 1}$$

Where fundamental parameters are defined as:

  • $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$ (Planck's Constant)
  • $c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$ (Speed of light)
  • $k_B = 1.380 \times 10^{-23} \text{ J/K}$ (Boltzmann's Constant)
  • $\lambda$ is wavelength in meters
  • $T$ is absolute temperature in Kelvin

2. Wien's Displacement Law

The peak wavelength ($\lambda_{\text{max}}$) where the spectral radiance reaches its absolute maximum shifts inversely with temperature:

$$\lambda_{\text{max}} \cdot T = b \approx 2.8977 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$$

3. The Stefan-Boltzmann Law

Integrating Planck's law across all wavelengths yields the total power density:

$$P/A = \sigma T^4$$

Where $\sigma \approx 5.670 \times 10^{-8} \text{ W}\cdot\text{m}^{-2}\cdot\text{K}^{-4}$ is the Stefan-Boltzmann constant.
