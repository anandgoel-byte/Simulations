Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What defines Row Echelon Form (REF)?

1. All rows consisting entirely of zeros are at the bottom.
2. The leading non-zero entry (pivot) of a row is strictly to the right of the pivot of the row above it.
3. All entries in a column below a pivot must be zero.
Question 2

What is the difference in Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)?

RREF meets all REF conditions, PLUS:
1. Every leading pivot must be exactly 1.
2. The pivot is the ONLY non-zero entry in its entire column (entries above the pivot are also zeroed out via back-substitution).
Question 3

What does a row like $[0\ 0\ 0 \mid 5]$ indicate?

This translates to the algebraic equation $0x + 0y + 0z = 5$. Since $0$ can never equal $5$, this represents an inconsistent system, meaning there is No Solution.
