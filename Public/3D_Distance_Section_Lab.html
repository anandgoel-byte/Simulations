Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

How is the 3D distance formula derived?

It is the Pythagorean Theorem applied twice. First across the flat XY plane to find the diagonal $r^2 = \Delta x^2 + \Delta y^2$. Then that diagonal is used with the height $\Delta z$ to find the true 3D distance: $d = \sqrt{r^2 + \Delta z^2}$.
Question 2

What happens to Point $P$ if the ratio $m:n$ is $1:1$ (Internal)?

If $m = n = 1$, the section formula perfectly collapses into the Midpoint Formula. The coordinates of $P$ become exactly the arithmetic average of $A$ and $B$: $\left(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2}, \frac{z_1+z_2}{2}\right)$.
Question 3

What does "External Division" physically mean?

It means Point $P$ lies on the straight line passing through $A$ and $B$, but outside the segment bounded by them. The ratio $m:n$ represents the distance from $P$ to $A$ compared to the distance from $P$ to $B$.
