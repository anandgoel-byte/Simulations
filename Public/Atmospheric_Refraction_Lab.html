🌎 ATMOSPHERIC OPTICS PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the Atmospheric Refraction Virtual Laboratory! Select a mode to begin exploring atmospheric physics.

💫 Mode A: Star Twinkling

Observe how concentric layers of atmosphere bend a star's light ray downwards, making the star appear higher in the sky. Slide the Turbulence control to animate convective thermal cells which perturb the ray and cause flickering.

🌅 Mode B: Early Sunrise

Rotate the Sun below the horizon. Watch light rays curve around the Earth's atmosphere to reach the observer. This accounts for seeing the Sun 2 minutes before it physically rises and 2 minutes after it sets!

🏜️ Mode C: Mirage Simulator

Explore temperature-driven refractive boundaries:

  • Inferior Mirage: Hot ground makes index lowest at the base. Rays curve upward, reflecting the sky onto the ground (creating puddle illusions).
  • Superior Mirage: Cold ground makes index highest at the base. Rays curve downward, projecting floating objects in the polar sky (Looming).
