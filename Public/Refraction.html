Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What is the mathematical relationship for the Minimum Angle of Deviation ($D_m$)?

The Prism Formula holds when the ray passes symmetrically ($i = e$): $$n = \frac{\sin\left(\frac{A + D_m}{2}\right)}{\sin\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}$$ Measuring $A$ and $D_m$ yields the refractive index $n$.
Question 2

Why does violet light bend more than red light?

According to Cauchy's Dispersion Equation, $n \approx A + B/\lambda^2$. Because violet light has a shorter wavelength ($\approx 400\text{ nm}$) compared to red ($\approx 700\text{ nm}$), it experiences a higher refractive index, thus bending more.
Question 3

When does Total Internal Reflection (TIR) occur?

TIR happens when the internal angle hitting the second face exceeds the critical angle $\theta_c = \arcsin(1/n)$. Dense materials (like Diamond) or wide apex angles easily trigger TIR.
