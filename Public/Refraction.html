Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What is the exact mathematical relationship between refractive index ($n$), Apex Angle ($A$), and Minimum Angle of Deviation ($D_m$)?

The Prism Formula holds when the ray passes symmetrically ($i = e$): $$n = \frac{\sin\left(\frac{A + D_m}{2}\right)}{\sin\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}$$ Under this unique condition, the refraction at both faces is balanced such that $r_1 = r_2 = \frac{A}{2}$. Measuring $A$ and $D_m$ via spectrometer yields $n$.
Question 2

What is the physical significance of the symmetrical light ray path inside the glass prism?

When the ray travels parallel to the base (symmetrically), the deviation of light $\delta$ reaches its absolute minimum value $D_m$. Mathematically: $$i = e \quad \text{and} \quad r_1 = r_2$$ Changing the incident angle in either direction from this state increases the total deviation angle.
Question 3

Why does blue or violet light bend more than red light during chromatic dispersion?

According to **Cauchy's Dispersion Equation**, the index of refraction $n$ depends on the incident wavelength $\lambda$: $$n(\lambda) \approx B + \frac{C}{\lambda^2}$$ Because violet light has a shorter wavelength ($\approx 400\text{ nm}$) compared to red light ($\approx 700\text{ nm}$), it experiences a higher refractive index $n$ in glass.
Question 4

Under what exact mathematical conditions will Total Internal Reflection (TIR) occur at the second interface?

TIR occurs when the internal angle of incidence on the second face ($r_2$) exceeds the critical angle ($\theta_c$) for the boundary: $$r_2 > \theta_c = \arcsin\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)$$ Since $r_2 = A - r_1$, a highly dense material (such as diamond, where $n = 2.42$, making $\theta_c \approx 24.4^\circ$) or a large Apex Angle $A$ will readily trigger total internal reflection.
Question 5

Can a prism propagate light with zero total deviation ($\delta = 0^\circ$)?

A single triangular glass prism of positive apex angle $A > 0^\circ$ surrounded by air ($n > 1$) cannot yield zero deviation because the entering and exiting faces are non-parallel. Zero net deviation requires placing an inverted second prism in contact (Direct Vision Prism setup).
