Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What is the difference between "Spring Equilibrium" and "System Equilibrium"?

Spring Equilibrium is the unstretched natural length of the spring ($y=0$), where $F_s = 0$.

System Equilibrium is where the net force is zero ($F_{net} = 0$). This occurs lower down when the upward spring force exactly cancels the downward gravitational weight: $$ky = mg \implies y_{eq} = \frac{mg}{k}$$
Question 2

At what position is the Kinetic Energy ($K$) of the mass at its absolute maximum?

Kinetic Energy is maximized when velocity is highest. This occurs exactly as the mass crosses the System Equilibrium point. At this point, the acceleration switches from positive to negative, meaning velocity has reached its peak.
Question 3

If the block is dropped from rest at the Spring Equilibrium, how far down will it fall?

Using conservation of energy from $y=0$ (where $v=0, U_s=0$) to the lowest point $y_{low}$ (where $v=0$ again): $$\Delta U_g + \Delta U_s = 0$$ $$-mg(y_{low}) + \frac{1}{2}k(y_{low})^2 = 0$$ $$y_{low} = \frac{2mg}{k}$$ It falls exactly twice the distance of the System Equilibrium!
Question 4

Does changing gravity ($g$) alter the period of oscillation?

No. The period of a mass-spring system is completely independent of gravity: $$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}$$ Gravity only shifts the central equilibrium point downwards, it does not change the restoring stiffness or the time it takes to complete one cycle.
* Physics verified via Symplectic Euler integrator. Constants: g = 9.80 m/s², Datum Ug=0 at y = -15m
