Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why is the resting potential negative, and what establishes this steady state?

Established by GHK membrane permeability and pump dynamics: $$V_m = \frac{RT}{F} \ln \left( \frac{P_{K}[K^+]_o + P_{Na}[Na^+]_o + P_{Cl}[Cl^-]_i}{P_{K}[K^+]_i + P_{Na}[Na^+]_i + P_{Cl}[Cl^-]_o} \right)$$ Leak channels allow $K^+$ to diffuse outward leaving net negative charges inside, while the electrogenic $3\text{ Na}^+ / 2\text{ K}^+$ pump maintains concentration gradients.
Question 2

Explain the structural "All-or-None" mechanism of Voltage-Gated Sodium Channels.

Sodium conductance scales via activation ($m$) and inactivation ($h$) gates: $$g_{Na} = \bar{g}_{Na} \cdot m^3 h$$ At rest, $m \to 0$ (closed). Depolarization above $-55\text{ mV}$ triggers cooperative opening ($m \to 1$). Within milliseconds, the ball-and-chain inactivation gate seals the pore ($h \to 0$), stopping inward current.
Question 3

What biophysical changes occur if Voltage-Gated Potassium channels are blocked?

Blocking $K^+$ channels (e.g. by TEA) prevents the active hyperpolarizing conductance: $$g_{K} = \bar{g}_{K} \cdot n^4 \to 0$$ This eliminates the rapid outward $K^+$ current, severely prolonging the action potential repolarization time, transforming the sharp spike into a wide plateau.
Question 4

How does Myelin sheath insulation alter axolemma conduction dynamics?

Myelination wraps the axon membrane, drastically decreasing capacitance ($C_m$) and increasing resistance. Velocity scales inversely with time constant: $$v \propto \frac{1}{\tau} \propto \frac{1}{R_a C_m}$$ Current travels instantly down insulated internodes, jumping to regenerate only at active Nodes of Ranvier (saltatory conduction).
Question 5

Explain the structural difference between absolute and relative refractory periods.

Absolute refractory occurs when $h \to 0$ due to complete inactivation of Na⁺ channels, making firing impossible. Relative refractory occurs during hyperpolarization overshoot; channels are closed but active $K^+$ efflux opposes new depolarization, requiring massive stimulus currents.
