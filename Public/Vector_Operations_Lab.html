Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What does the Dot Product represent geometrically?

The dot product is a scalar (a number). It represents how much of vector $\vec{u}$ points in the exact same direction as $\vec{v}$. It's calculated as $|\vec{u}| |\vec{v}| \cos(\theta)$. If the dot product is exactly 0, the vectors are perfectly orthogonal (perpendicular).
Question 2

Why does the Cross Product output a new vector?

Unlike the dot product, the cross product generates a third vector that is perpendicular to the plane formed by $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$. The length (magnitude) of this new vector is exactly equal to the area of the parallelogram spanned by $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$.
Question 3

How does Vector Addition work visually?

It uses the "Tip-to-Tail" or "Parallelogram" rule. If you imagine walking along vector $\vec{u}$, and then walking along vector $\vec{v}$ from that new spot, the Resultant vector is the straight-line path from your start to your finish.
