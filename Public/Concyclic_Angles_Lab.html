Class 9 Theorems
Central Angle Theorem Theorem 10.8
The angle subtended by an arc at the center is double the angle subtended by it at any remaining part of the circle.
Relationship: $\angle AOB = 2\angle ACB$
Angles in Same Segment Theorem 10.9
Angles in the same segment of a circle are equal.
Relationship: $\angle ACB = \angle ADB$
Cyclic Quadrilateral Theorem 10.11
The sum of either pair of opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral is $180^\circ$.
Relationship: $\angle A + \angle C = 180^\circ$

📘 CONCYCLICITY LAB PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the Concyclicity & Arc Angles Virtual Laboratory! Explore the geometric relations between central angles, inscribed angles, segment segments, and concyclic systems.

🎯 Core Sandbox Controls

  • Base Arc Size: Resize the pink arc AB symmetrically using the top range slider or by dragging Points A or B directly on the canvas.
  • Drag Points C & D: Move these points anywhere along the major arc. Notice that the inscribed angles ($\angle ACB$ and $\angle ADB$) remain constant, proving they are always equal in the same segment.
  • Toggles: Use the checkboxes at the top to toggle the circumcircle outline and the central angle ($\angle AOB$) vectors.
  • Holographic Zoom: Use the zoom controls (+, -, RST) in the top right to scale the physics canvas.

📊 Telemetry Dashboard

  • Central Angle: Displays the exact angle $\angle AOB$ subtended by the arc at the center.
  • Inscribed Angles: Tracks $\angle ACB$ and $\angle ADB$. Verify that the central angle is always exactly double the inscribed angle.
  • Concyclic State: Confirms that points A, B, C, and D lie on a single circular path, making them concyclic.
