Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What does Eccentricity ($e$) actually measure geometrically?

Eccentricity is the constant ratio of the distance from any point on the curve to the Focus, compared to its perpendicular distance to the Directrix line.
Question 2

Why does the Ellipse become a perfect Circle when $e=0$?

When $e$ approaches 0, the two foci collapse inward and merge exactly at the center. Simultaneously, the directrix lines ($a/e$) shoot outwards to infinity. The curve becomes perfectly symmetric!
Question 3

What happens precisely at the boundary where $e=1$?

The curve "breaks open". The ellipse stretches infinitely until it snaps into a Parabola. It no longer loops back on itself. As $e$ increases past 1, the curve bends backwards, forming the two mirrored branches of a Hyperbola.
* Semi-major axis ($a$) is fixed to scale the curves visibly on the canvas. Parabola boundary smoothed mathematically.

📘 CONIC SECTIONS PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the Eccentricity Laboratory! Explore how a single parameter dictates the shape of orbits, satellite dishes, and architectural curves.

🎯 Core Controls

  • Eccentricity Slider ($e$): Drag this to smoothly transform the shape from a perfect Circle ($e=0$) to a stretched Ellipse ($e < 1$) and finally into a split Hyperbola ($e > 1$).
  • Foci ($F$): The focal points. Watch them move outward from the center as eccentricity increases!
  • Directrices: The vertical bounding lines. Watch them snap from infinity down toward the origin.
  • Asymptotes: Only visible when $e > 1$. These are the diagonal lines the hyperbola branches approach at infinity.
