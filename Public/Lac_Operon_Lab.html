📘 GENE REGULATION PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the Molecular Genetics Laboratory! Explore how bacteria turn genes on and off based on their environment.

🎯 Core Controls

  • Lactose Slider: Controls the inducer. When lactose is high, watch it bind to the Red Repressor protein, changing its shape and forcing it to release its grip on the DNA Operator.
  • Glucose Slider: Glucose suppresses cAMP. When glucose is LOW, the cell produces cAMP (blue dots), which bind to the CAP protein. Active CAP binds to the DNA and acts as a massive booster for RNA Polymerase.
  • Transcription Rate: The ultimate goal! Watch the purple RNA Polymerase slide down the DNA to create mRNA strands. It is blocked by the Repressor, weak without CAP, and extremely fast when CAP is bound and the Repressor is gone!
