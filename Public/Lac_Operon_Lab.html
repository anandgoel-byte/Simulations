📘 LAC OPERON NCERT CORE STUDY SHEET

This laboratory models the lac operon of Escherichia coli based strictly on the NCERT Class 12 Biology textbook curriculum for CBSE & NEET aspirants.

🧬 Gene Sequence ($i - p - o - z - y - a$)

  • $i$ (Inhibitor / Regulator): Constitutively produces repressor protein.
  • $p$ (Promoter): Binding site for RNA Polymerase.
  • $o$ (Operator): Adjacent to promoter; binds the active repressor protein.
  • Structural Genes ($z, y, a$): Transcribed as a single polycistronic mRNA.
    • $lacZ$ ($\beta$-galactosidase): Hydrolyzes lactose into Glucose and Galactose.
    • $lacY$ (Permease): Increases membrane permeability to allow lactose entry.
    • $lacA$ (Transacetylase): Catalyzes transacetylation reactions.

🔄 Negative Regulation (Inducer Control)

  • Lactose Absent: Repressor binds to Operator ($o$), physically blocking RNA Polymerase. Transcription is shut down (OFF).
  • Lactose Present (Induction): Lactose (acting as inducer) binds to the repressor, changing its shape. The inactive repressor falls off the Operator, allowing transcription to begin (ON).
  • Basal Expression Requirement: Lactose cannot enter E. coli unless permease channels are already active. A tiny basal level of transcription is always active in the cell to maintain permease channels.
