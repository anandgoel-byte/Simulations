Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why does Hess's Law mathematically work?

Enthalpy ($H$) is a State Function. This means the change in enthalpy ($\Delta H$) depends strictly on the initial and final states of the system, completely independent of the physical pathway taken to get there.
Question 2

If Step 1 is highly endothermic (+200 kJ) and Step 2 is highly exothermic (-300 kJ), what is the overall reaction type?

You simply add them: $+200 \text{ kJ} + (-300 \text{ kJ}) = -100 \text{ kJ}$. Since the net enthalpy change is negative, the overall reaction is Exothermic. It releases more energy forming the final products than it absorbed to form the intermediates.
Question 3

What happens to $\Delta H$ if a specific reaction step is written in reverse?

The magnitude of $\Delta H$ remains exactly the same, but the sign is flipped. If it cost energy to break bonds in the forward direction (+), that exact same amount of energy will be released (-) when those bonds reform in the reverse direction.
Question 4

If the stoichiometric coefficients of a step are multiplied by a factor of 2, how is $\Delta H$ affected?

Enthalpy is an Extensive Property. If you double the amount of matter reacting (multiplying coefficients by 2), you generate or absorb exactly twice as much heat. Therefore, $\Delta H$ must also be multiplied by 2.
