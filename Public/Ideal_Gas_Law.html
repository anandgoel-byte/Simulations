Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Boyle's Law: If you halve the volume ($V$) while keeping temperature ($T$) and moles ($n$) constant, what happens to the pressure ($P$)?

According to Boyle's Law, $P \propto \frac{1}{V}$. Therefore, halving the volume causes the pressure to double, as particles collide with the walls twice as frequently.
Question 2

Charles's Law: Why must temperature be measured in Kelvin when calculating gas volumes?

Charles's Law states $V \propto T$. If Celsius were used, $0^\circ\text{C}$ would imply zero volume, which is physically impossible. Kelvin sets Absolute Zero ($0\text{ K}$) where all particle kinetic motion ceases.
Question 3

What are the two main assumptions of the Kinetic Molecular Theory for an *ideal* gas?

1) Gas particles themselves have negligible physical volume compared to the container.

2) There are no intermolecular attractive or repulsive forces between the particles (perfectly elastic collisions).
Question 4

Under what real-world conditions do gases deviate most from ideal behavior?

Gases deviate at Low Temperatures and High Pressures. When squeezed tightly and moving slowly, the physical volume of the particles and the intermolecular attractions can no longer be ignored.

📘 IDEAL GAS LAW PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the 3D Ideal Gas Law PVT Simulator! Explore the kinetic molecular theory by interacting with a closed thermodynamic system.

🎯 Core Controls

  • Moles ($n$): Adjusts the total number of gas particles in the chamber.
  • Volume ($V$): Moves the 3D piston up and down, changing the total available space.
  • Temperature ($T$): Modifies the kinetic energy (speed) of the particles and visually alters the thermal heater plate color.
  • Pressure ($P$): This is a dependent variable. Watch the live readout to see how $P$ responds dynamically to changes in $V$, $T$, or $n$ based on $PV=nRT$.
  • Camera Controls: Click and drag on the 3D viewport to orbit around the chamber. Scroll to zoom in and out.
