📘 MAGNETIC PROPERTIES PROTOCOL

Welcome to the Magnetic Materials 3D Simulator! Observe how atomic dipoles react to external fields and heat.

🎯 Core Controls

  • Diamagnetic: Notice that when $B_{ext} = 0$, there are no dipoles. When you apply a field, tiny arrows appear pointing exactly opposite to the field.
  • Paramagnetic: Atoms have permanent dipoles, but they point randomly due to thermal jitter. When you apply a field, they try to align upwards, but the Temperature (T) slider will randomize them (Curie's Law).
  • Ferromagnetic: Atoms are locked together in chunks called Magnetic Domains. Applying a field causes massive, rigid alignment. Even if you drop $B_{ext}$ back to 0, they retain some alignment (Hysteresis), unless you crank the Temperature past the Curie Point!

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Magnetic Phase Space: Understand how Diamagnetic, Paramagnetic, and Ferromagnetic materials differ at the quantum/atomic level.
  • Curie's Law & Hysteresis: Observe how thermal energy breaks magnetic alignment, and how domain walls exhibit memory (hysteresis).
  • Magnetic Susceptibility ($\chi$): Study the sign and magnitude of $\chi$ and how it defines repulsion vs attraction.
