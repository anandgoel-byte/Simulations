The Control Center

Nucleus

A double-membraned structure housing the cell's genetic material (DNA). It manages protein synthesis and cell replication.

NEET High-Yield Fact

Robert Brown discovered the nucleus in 1831. The nucleolus is the active site for ribosomal RNA (rRNA) synthesis.

🎨 Cell Organelle Analogy Map

A cell is like a highly organized industrial city. Each organelle plays a dedicated functional role to ensure the cell's survival, manufacturing products, generating energy, and managing waste.

Nucleus & Ribosomes

Town Hall & Factories

The **Nucleus** acts as the city's headquarters (holding the blue-prints/DNA). **Ribosomes** act as manufacturing facilities, translating these blueprint templates into protein building blocks.

Mitochondria

Thermal Power Station

The **Mitochondria** acts as the power station, generating chemical fuel in the form of ATP through cellular respiration, supplying power to all metabolic functions.

ER & Golgi Complex

Highways & Post Office

The **Endoplasmic Reticulum** provides assembly and internal transport highway systems. The **Golgi Apparatus** serves as the packaging and shipping department, adding chemical labels (glycosylation) before delivery.

Lysosomes & Vacuoles

Incinerators & Warehouses

**Lysosomes** act as trash collectors and incinerators (using acidic digestive enzymes). **Vacuoles** act as central reservoirs and warehouse storage units for raw materials, sap, and water.
