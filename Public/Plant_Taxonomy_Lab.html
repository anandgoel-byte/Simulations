📘 TAXONOMY PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the Floral Topology Explorer! Botanists use cross-sectional "Floral Diagrams" alongside "Floral Formulas" to classify plants into families.

🌿 Reading the Visual Map

  • Calyx (Green): The outermost whorl of sepals. If the arcs touch, they are fused (Gamosepalous).
  • Corolla (Pink): The petals. Note the aestivation (how they overlap). Fabaceae has a highly unique "butterfly" setup!
  • Androecium (Yellow): The stamens (male organs). Notice how they are clustered. Solanaceae attaches them directly to the petals (Epipetalous).
  • Gynoecium (Cyan): The ovary in the dead center. The lines inside show placentation (where the seeds attach).
