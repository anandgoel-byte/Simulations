Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What does ELI the ICE man mean in AC circuits?

It's a mnemonic to remember phase shifts!

ELI: In an Inductor (L), voltage (E) leads current (I) by 90°.
ICE: In a Capacitor (C), current (I) leads voltage (E) by 90°.
Question 2

What happens mathematically to the circuit at the Resonance Frequency ($f_0$)?

At resonance, the inductive reactance ($X_L$) exactly equals the capacitive reactance ($X_C$). Because they are 180° out of phase, they completely cancel each other out ($X_L - X_C = 0$). The total impedance $Z$ drops to its minimum value (just $R$), and current reaches its maximum peak.
Question 3

Why is a Power Factor ($\cos \phi$) of 1.0 desirable in power grids?

Real power (Watts) actually consumed by a circuit is $P = V \times I \times \cos(\phi)$. If the phase angle $\phi$ is large, $\cos(\phi)$ approaches 0, meaning the utility company has to pump massive amounts of current (I) through the wires to deliver the same real power. This wastes energy as heat in the transmission lines.
Question 4

How do you graphically add $V_R$, $V_L$, and $V_C$ to find total Voltage $V$ on the Phasor Diagram?

You add them via vector addition! Because $V_L$ points 90° ahead and $V_C$ points 90° behind the current, they lie on the same axis but point opposite ways. You subtract them ($V_L - V_C$) to get the net reactive voltage, and then use the Pythagorean theorem with $V_R$ to find the hypotenuse ($V_{total}$).
* Current (I) is visually scaled up relative to Voltage for clear representation on graphs.
