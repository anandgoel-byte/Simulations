📘 RAY OPTICS PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the Geometrical Optics Laboratory! Explore how spherical mirrors manipulate light rays to form images.

🎯 Core Controls

  • Mirror Type: Switch between Concave (Converging, forms both real and virtual images) and Convex (Diverging, forms only virtual diminished images).
  • Object Distance Slider: Move the blue object arrow. Pay close attention to what happens in a Concave mirror as you cross the Center of Curvature ($C$) and the Focal Point ($F$)!
  • Ray Tracing: The simulator dynamically traces two standard rays: the Parallel Ray (reflects through Focus) and the Pole Ray (reflects symmetrically). Where they intersect is where the image forms!
  • Sign Convention: Notice that objects are on the left ($-x$), so $u$ is always negative. Real images form on the left ($-v$), while virtual images form behind the mirror on the right ($+v$).
