Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What does the Modulus $|z|$ represent geometrically?

The modulus $|z| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ is the exact Euclidean distance from the origin $(0,0)$ to the point $(x,y)$ on the Argand plane. It is the geometric length of the vector.
Question 2

How does Multiplication work visually on this plane?

In polar form, $z_1 \cdot z_2 = (r_1 r_2)e^{i(\theta_1 + \theta_2)}$. Geometrically, you multiply their lengths together, and you add their angles together. Multiplying by $i$ simply rotates a vector by $90^\circ$ counter-clockwise!
Question 3

What is the Complex Conjugate ($\overline{z}$)?

The complex conjugate of $z = x + iy$ is $\overline{z} = x - iy$. Visually, it is the exact mirror reflection of the vector across the horizontal Real ($\Re$) axis.
