Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why do the slope lines form a pattern but never cross each other?

Because $f(x,y)$ evaluates to a single, unique value for any specific coordinate $(x,y)$. A point cannot have two different slopes at the exact same time. This is a visual representation of the Uniqueness Theorem for differential equations!
Question 2

What role does the Initial Condition $(x_0, y_0)$ play?

The general differential equation represents a whole "family" of infinite parallel curves. The initial condition acts as an anchor—it specifies exactly which one of those infinite curves we are looking at by forcing the solution to pass through that specific point.
Question 3

What does a completely horizontal slope line ($\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$) indicate?

A slope of zero means the function is not changing ($y$ is constant). If there is a continuous horizontal line of zero slopes across the graph, it represents an Equilibrium Solution (e.g., $y=4$ in Logistic Growth where the population maxes out).
