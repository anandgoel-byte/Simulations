Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why does the chamber turn a darker brown when you increase the Volume?

Increasing volume decreases the overall pressure. Le Chatelier's Principle states the system will shift to counteract this by producing more moles of gas. Since the reactants have 2 moles ($2\text{NO}_2$) and products have 1 mole ($\text{N}_2\text{O}_4$), it shifts left, producing more brown $\text{NO}_2$.
Question 2

What happens to the target Equilibrium Constant ($K_c$) when you increase Temperature?

Because this reaction is Exothermic (releases heat), treating "heat" as a product means adding heat pushes the reaction backward (left). Therefore, the value of $K_c$ mathematically decreases at higher temperatures.
Question 3

If you inject extra $\text{NO}_2$, how does the system respond on the graph?

You will see an immediate vertical spike in $[\text{NO}_2]$. Because $Q_c$ is now lower than $K_c$, the forward reaction accelerates. The graph will show $[\text{NO}_2]$ curving back down while $[\text{N}_2\text{O}_4]$ curves up until a new equilibrium is established.
Question 4

When the system is perfectly at equilibrium, are the molecules stationary?

No! Chemical equilibrium is Dynamic. $\text{NO}_2$ molecules are constantly colliding to form $\text{N}_2\text{O}_4$, and $\text{N}_2\text{O}_4$ molecules are constantly splitting apart. It is simply that the rate of the forward reaction perfectly equals the rate of the reverse reaction, so net concentrations remain constant.
