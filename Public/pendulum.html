Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

How does the period of oscillation change if you double the bob mass ($m$)?

The restoring torque is $\tau = -mgL \sin\theta$. Using Newton's 2nd Law for rotation ($\tau = I \alpha$ where $I = mL^2$): $$-mgL\sin\theta = mL^2 \frac{d^2\theta}{dt^2} \implies \frac{d^2\theta}{dt^2} + \frac{g}{L}\sin\theta = 0$$ Mass $m$ cancels linearly on both sides, yielding a mass-independent period: $$T_0 = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$
Question 2

Why does a pendulum released at large angles take longer than the small-angle prediction?

Small-angle approximation assumes $\sin\theta \approx \theta$. At large releases, $\sin\theta < \theta$ holds, meaning restoring forces are weaker than expected. Exact integration via elliptic integrals yields: $$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}\left(1 + \frac{1}{4}\sin^2\frac{\theta_0}{2} + \frac{9}{64}\sin^4\frac{\theta_0}{2} + \dots \right)$$
Question 3

What are the values of velocity and acceleration when the bob is at maximum deflection?

At maximum deflection $\theta_0$, the bob stops to reverse direction, making velocity zero: $$v = L\omega = 0 \implies \text{KE} = 0$$ Tangential acceleration is maximized under peak gravity restoring force: $$a_t = -g\sin\theta_0$$ Centripetal acceleration is zero: $a_c = L\omega^2 = 0$.
Question 4

Why is the string tension greater than the static weight of the bob at the lowest point?

At the vertical equilibrium point ($\theta = 0$), the bob sweeps along a circular path of radius $L$ at velocity $v$. Net radial acceleration is centripetal, supplied by the difference between Tension and Gravity: $$\Sigma F_r = T - mg = m \frac{v^2}{L} \implies T = mg + m\frac{v^2}{L}$$
Question 5

How does transporting the pendulum to the Moon affect its period?

The restorative gravity torque scales down in the Moon's weaker gravity field ($g_{moon} \approx 1.62 \text{ m/s}^2$). Since period scales inversely with acceleration: $$T \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{g}} \implies T_{moon} \approx \sqrt{\frac{9.8}{1.62}} \cdot T_{earth} \approx 2.46 \cdot T_{earth}$$
