Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why does Water ($H_2O$) have a bond angle of $104.5^\circ$ instead of $109.5^\circ$?

Under VSEPR, electrostatic repulsion varies by pair structure: $$\text{Repulsion: } \text{LP-LP} > \text{LP-BP} > \text{BP-BP}$$ Oxygen holds 2 bonding pairs and 2 massive lone pairs ($\text{AX}_2\text{E}_2$). These lone clouds expand in coordinate space, compressing the O-H angles: $$\theta_{\text{H-O-H}} = 109.5^\circ - 2 \times 2.5^\circ = 104.5^\circ$$
Question 2

How do Period 3 elements like Sulfur support expanded octets up to 12 electrons?

Period 3 elements have a principal quantum number $n = 3$, meaning the $3d$ shell is energetically accessible: $$S^*: [Ne]\, 3s^1 3p^3 3d^2 \rightarrow d^2sp^3 \text{ hybrid}$$ This promotions grid permits up to 6 spatial bonds (e.g., $SF_6$, octahedral geometry). Period 2 elements are strictly limited to $s, p$ subshells.
Question 3

Why do lone pairs occupy equatorial coordinates in a Trigonal Bipyramid ($\text{SN} = 5$)?

Equatorial spots offer wider spatial angles ($120^\circ$) than axial spots ($90^\circ$). Because: $$\text{Repulsion at } 90^\circ \gg \text{Repulsion at } 120^\circ$$ equatorial coordinates experience significantly less total electrostatic potential energy, shielding the lone lobes.
Question 4

How do multiple (double/triple) bonds influence VSEPR geometries?

A multiple bond is treated as a single high-density coordinate domain. Due to greater physical $\pi$-overlays, they repel single bonds strongly: $$\text{Repulsion: } \text{Triple} > \text{Double} > \text{Single}$$ In formaldehyde ($H_2CO$), the $C=O$ double bond compresses the $H-C-H$ angle to $116^\circ$.
Question 5

Why is Xenon Tetrafluoride ($XeF_4$) planar even with Octahedral electron groups?

$XeF_4$ has a steric number of 6 with 2 lone pairs ($\text{AX}_4\text{E}_2$). To minimize LP-LP repulsions, the lone clouds sit at opposite poles ($180^\circ$): $$\theta_{\text{LP-Xe-LP}} = 180^\circ$$ This leaves the four fluorine atoms balanced in the flat equatorial plane at $90^\circ$ angles.
