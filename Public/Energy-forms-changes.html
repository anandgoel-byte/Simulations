Question 1

How does the Energy Forms & Changes suite demonstrate the First Law of Thermodynamics?

The First Law states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed: $$\Delta U = Q - W$$ In the Kettle-Generator-Beaker system, thermal energy of steam ($Q_{\text{steam}}$) transforms into mechanical work ($W_{\text{wheel}}$), which converts to electrical energy ($E_e$), and finally dissipates as thermal heat in the beaker: $$\sum E_{\text{in}} = \sum E_{\text{out}}$$
Question 2

Why does a photovoltaic solar cell fail to extract energy from a bicycle wheel or water faucet?

Solar cells operate on the **photoelectric effect**. Photons with energy $h\nu$ greater than the semiconductor bandgap $E_g$ excite valence electrons to the conduction band: $$h\nu \ge E_g$$ Kinetic streams (water/steam) or thermal vibrations have energies in the sub-eV range, which are far below the silicon bandgap threshold ($E_g \approx 1.11\text{ eV}$), yielding zero quantum conversion: $$\text{Quantum Yield } \Phi = 0$$
Question 3

Why does a Fluorescent CFL bulb operate at a much higher luminous efficacy than an Incandescent bulb?

The luminous efficiency $\eta$ is the ratio of visible light power output to total electrical power input. Incandescent bulbs emit light via blackbody radiation at $T \approx 2800\text{ K}$, where the peak emission is in the infrared spectrum: $$\lambda_{\text{peak}} T = b \implies \lambda_{\text{peak}} \approx 1000\text{ nm}$$ This results in $\approx 90\%$ lost as heat ($E_{th}$). CFLs excite mercury vapor to emit UV light which is converted by phosphor coatings, yielding higher visible efficacy: $$\eta_{\text{CFL}} \approx 4.5 \times \eta_{\text{incandescent}}$$
Question 4

Why can we never achieve exactly 100% conversion efficiency in any of these setups?

Mechanical friction and electrical resistance inevitably degrade some energy into thermal heat (entropy increase), as dictated by the Second Law of Thermodynamics: $$\Delta S_{\text{universe}} > 0$$ This means absolute 100% mechanical-to-electrical conversion is physically impossible.
