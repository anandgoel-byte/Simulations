Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What separates during Anaphase of Mitosis?

During Anaphase of Mitosis, the centromeres split and Sister Chromatids are pulled apart to opposite poles of the cell. This ensures each new daughter cell receives an identical diploid ($2n$) set of chromosomes.
Question 2

How does Metaphase I of Meiosis differ from Metaphase of Mitosis?

In Mitosis, individual chromosomes line up single-file along the metaphase plate. In Meiosis I, chromosomes line up in Homologous Pairs (tetrads/bivalents) alongside each other, preparing to reduce the ploidy level of the cell.
Question 3

What happens to the DNA content (C value) during Meiosis II?

The cell enters Meiosis II already haploid ($n$) but with replicated chromosomes ($2C$ DNA content). During Anaphase II, the sister chromatids finally separate. The resulting four gametes are haploid ($n$) and have exactly half the DNA mass ($1C$).
