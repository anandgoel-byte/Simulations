Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

How does a three-fold increase in Temperature impact Pressure in a closed, rigid container?

According to Gay-Lussac's Law, gas pressure is directly proportional to absolute temperature at constant volume: $$P \propto T \quad (\text{constant } V, N)$$ $$\frac{P_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2}{T_2}$$ If $T_2 = 3T_1$, then $P_2 = 3P_1$. The increase in thermal energy raises the average velocity of gas molecules, resulting in higher momentum transfer during wall collisions.
Question 2

Why do light Helium (He) atoms move faster than Heavy Nitrogen ($N_2$) molecules at the same Temperature?

Temperature is a macroscopic measure of average kinetic energy: $$KE_{\text{avg}} = \frac{3}{2}k_B T = \frac{1}{2}m v_{\text{rms}}^2$$ Solving for root-mean-square velocity yields: $$v_{\text{rms}} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}}$$ Since the molar mass $M_{\text{He}} \approx 4\text{ g/mol}$ is much smaller than $M_{\text{N}_2} \approx 28\text{ g/mol}$, helium possesses a higher average speed to maintain equivalent kinetic energy.
Question 3

How does halving the container volume at constant Temperature alter system Pressure?

Boyle's Law establishes that pressure is inversely proportional to volume at constant temperature: $$P \propto \frac{1}{V} \quad (\text{constant } T, N)$$ $$P_1 V_1 = P_2 V_2$$ If $V_2 = 0.5 V_1$, then $P_2 = 2P_1$. Crowding gas particles into half the physical volume doubles the frequency of collisions against the container walls, doubling pressure.
Question 4

According to KMT, what is the direct physical cause of gas pressure?

Gas pressure is defined as the force exerted by gas particles colliding with container walls per unit area: $$P = \frac{F}{A}$$ Each time a particle of mass $m$ bounces elastically off a wall, it transfers momentum: $$\Delta p = 2 m v_x$$ Summing these impacts over time $t$ gives the net force: $$F = \frac{\sum \Delta p_i}{\Delta t}$$ Inter-molecular collisions exchange energy but exert no net pressure against the boundaries.
Question 5

Why is the Maxwell-Boltzmann speed distribution curve asymmetric?

The Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution probability density function is: $$f(v) = 4\pi \left(\frac{m}{2\pi k_B T}\right)^{3/2} v^2 e^{-\frac{m v^2}{2k_B T}}$$ It features a right-skewed tail because: 1. Speeds cannot drop below zero ($v \ge 0$). 2. High-speed components decline exponentially due to the kinetic factor $e^{-\frac{mv^2}{2k_BT}}$, creating a long tail as speed approaches infinity.
