Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

If you double the volume of the solution by adding water, what happens to the Molarity?

Molarity is inversely proportional to volume ($M = n/V$). If volume is doubled ($2V$) while moles ($n$) remain constant, the new concentration becomes exactly half of the original. This is the principle of dilution.
Question 2

What physically happens in the beaker when the solution reaches "Saturation"?

Saturation is the maximum concentration limit for a specific solute in a solvent at a given temperature. Once reached, adding more solute will not increase the molarity; the excess solute will simply precipitate and pile up as a solid at the bottom of the beaker.
Question 3

During evaporation, why does the color of the solution become darker?

Evaporation removes the solvent (water, decreasing $V$) but leaves the solute particles ($n$) behind. As $V$ decreases, the ratio $n/V$ increases, resulting in a higher molarity. Higher concentration absorbs more light, making the solution visually darker or more opaque.
Question 4

Write the Dilution Equation and explain its components.

$$M_1 V_1 = M_2 V_2$$ Because moles of solute remain constant during dilution ($n = M \cdot V$), the initial molarity times initial volume must equal the final molarity times final volume.
