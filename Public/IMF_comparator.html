Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why does Water ($H_2O$) have a much higher boiling point than Neon, despite being similar in size?

Water molecules experience Hydrogen Bonding, an exceptionally strong dipole-dipole interaction occurring when Hydrogen is covalently bonded to highly electronegative Oxygen. Neon only experiences weak, temporary London Dispersion Forces. It takes significantly more thermal energy ($T$) to break Hydrogen bonds.
Question 2

What physically causes London Dispersion Forces in non-polar molecules like Neon?

They are caused by random, instantaneous fluctuations in the electron cloud distribution. When electrons momentarily gather on one side, it creates a temporary dipole ($\delta^-$ and $\delta^+$), which induces a complementary dipole in a neighboring molecule, causing weak attraction.
Question 3

At a microscopic level, what is the difference between a Solid, Liquid, and Gas?

Solid: $E_{IMF} \gg E_{Kinetic}$. Molecules are locked in a rigid lattice by unbroken bonds.
Liquid: $E_{IMF} \approx E_{Kinetic}$. Bonds break and reform rapidly; molecules stay clumped but slide past each other.
Gas: $E_{Kinetic} > E_{IMF}$. Thermal energy dominates, bonds are broken, and molecules fly freely apart.
