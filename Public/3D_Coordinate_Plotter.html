Question 1

What are the "Octants" in 3D geometry?

In 2D, the X and Y axes divide the plane into 4 Quadrants. In 3D, the three mutually perpendicular planes (XY, YZ, XZ) divide space into 8 distinct regions called Octants. Octant I is where all three coordinates (x, y, z) are positive.
Question 2

What do the dashed projection lines represent visually?

They form a rectangular cuboid. The dashed lines show how the point $P(x,y,z)$ projects perpendicularly onto the three base coordinate planes (e.g., dropping straight down to the XY plane). This helps visualize its exact depth, height, and width in space.
Question 3

How is the 3D distance formula derived?

It's just the Pythagorean Theorem applied twice! First, find the distance across the flat XY floor: $r^2 = x^2 + y^2$. Then, use that diagonal as the base of a new triangle rising up the Z-axis: $d^2 = r^2 + z^2$. Substituting gives $d = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}$.

📘 3D PLOTTER PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the 3D Coordinate Laboratory! Explore the geometry of points in three-dimensional space.

🎯 Core Controls

  • X, Y, Z Sliders: Adjust the individual coordinates of point $P$. Watch how it moves relative to the three primary axes!
  • Camera Controls: The 3D canvas is fully interactive. Click and drag to orbit the camera around the origin. Scroll to zoom in and out.
  • Projections Toggle: Generates the dashed "cuboid" connecting the point to the floor and walls, helping you visually grasp its depth and height.
  • Vector Toggle: Draws the direct position vector $\vec{r}$ pointing straight from the origin to the point.
