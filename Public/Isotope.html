📘 ISOTOPE LAB PROTOCOLS

Welcome to the Isotopes & Atomic Mass Laboratory! Explore nuclide structure, stability limits, and weighted mixture calculations.

🎯 Core Controls

  • Isotope Builder Sandbox: Select an element, then adjust Protons ($Z$), Neutrons ($N$), and Electrons ($e^-$) to assemble stable or unstable nuclei.
  • Isotope Mixtures & Abundance: Adjust counts of isotopes A, B, and C to compute a weighted average mass and compare it on the balance scale.
  • Target Element Bar: Quickly jump between Hydrogen, Helium, Carbon, Oxygen, Bromine, and more.
  • Reset Lab: Restore the current element to its default stable configuration.

🎓 Learning Outcomes

  • Isotopic Structure: Understand how proton and neutron counts define mass number and nuclear stability.
  • Natural Abundance: Relate decimal periodic-table masses to the relative abundance of stable isotopes.
  • Weighted Averages: Compute mixture atomic masses using $M_{\text{avg}} = \sum n_i m_i / \sum n_i$.
