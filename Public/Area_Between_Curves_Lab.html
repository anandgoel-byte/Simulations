Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why do we integrate $f(x) - g(x)$ specifically?

The integral sums up infinitely many vertical slices (rectangles). The height of any individual slice is precisely the top $y$-value minus the bottom $y$-value. Therefore, the height function is $h(x) = f(x) - g(x)$.
Question 2

What happens to the calculation if the two curves cross each other?

If the curves cross, the "top" and "bottom" functions switch! If you just integrate $f(x) - g(x)$, the area where $g(x) > f(x)$ will evaluate as negative, canceling out actual geometric area. To find true total area, you must split the integral at the intersection points.
Question 3

What does the Riemann Rectangles toggle illustrate?

It reveals the fundamental definition of the integral: $\lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^{n} [f(x_i) - g(x_i)]\Delta x$. By breaking the area into discrete rectangles, it visualizes how calculus bridges the gap between finite sums and continuous areas.
* Numerical area is calculated using a high-precision discrete summation approximation over the interval $[a, b]$.
