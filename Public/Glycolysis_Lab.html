Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why does the cell initially spend 2 ATP in Steps 1 and 3?

This is the Energy Investment Phase. Phosphorylating the glucose molecule traps it inside the cell (due to the negative charge) and destabilizes the sugar, making it much easier for Aldolase to cleave it in half during Step 4.
Question 2

Which step is considered the primary "committed step" of Glycolysis?

Step 3 (catalyzed by Phosphofructokinase, PFK). This reaction is highly exergonic and essentially irreversible. Once the cell creates Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate, it is committed to finishing the glycolysis pathway. PFK is heavily regulated by allosteric feedback.
Question 3

Why do we get 4 ATP back in the Payoff Phase if only two reactions make ATP?

At Step 4, the 6-carbon sugar is split into two 3-carbon molecules (G3P). From Step 6 onwards, every reaction happens twice per original glucose molecule. Thus, the two ATP-producing steps (Step 7 and 10) yield $2 \times 1 + 2 \times 1 = 4$ ATP total!
