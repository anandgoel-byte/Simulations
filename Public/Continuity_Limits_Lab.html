Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What are the three strict conditions for continuity at a point?

1. The function is defined at the point: $f(c)$ exists.
2. The overall limit exists: $\lim_{x \to c} f(x)$ exists (meaning LHL = RHL).
3. The limit equals the function value: $\lim_{x \to c} f(x) = f(c)$.
Question 2

What is the visual difference between a Jump and a Removable discontinuity?

A Removable discontinuity (hole) occurs when the LHL and RHL perfectly match, but $f(c)$ is either undefined or displaced elsewhere. A Jump discontinuity occurs when the LHL and RHL evaluate to two completely different finite numbers, physically breaking the curve.
Question 3

Can a function have limits from both sides but still be discontinuous?

Yes, absolutely! If LHL and RHL exist but do not equal each other, it's a Jump discontinuity. If they DO equal each other but do not equal $f(c)$, it's a Removable discontinuity. The existence of limits alone does not guarantee continuity.
