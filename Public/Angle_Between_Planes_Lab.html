Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

Why do we use the Normal Vectors to find the angle?

The angle between two intersecting flat planes is geometrically identical to the angle between their perpendicular normal vectors. Vectors are much easier to manipulate algebraically using the Dot Product than infinite flat surfaces!
Question 2

What happens if the Dot Product is exactly zero?

If $\vec{n_1} \cdot \vec{n_2} = 0$, then the numerator is 0, making $\cos(\theta) = 0$. This means $\theta = 90^\circ$. The two planes are perfectly Orthogonal (perpendicular) to each other!
Question 3

How is the Line of Intersection determined mathematically?

The line of intersection lies strictly inside BOTH planes. Therefore, its direction vector must be completely perpendicular to both normal vectors. We find this by taking the Cross Product: $\vec{v_{line}} = \vec{n_1} \times \vec{n_2}$.
* The absolute value $|\vec{n_1} \cdot \vec{n_2}|$ is used to ensure we always calculate the Acute angle ($0^\circ$ to $90^\circ$) between the planes.
