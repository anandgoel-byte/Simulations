Conceptual Assessments

Question 1

What is the fundamental difference between a Latch and a Flip-Flop?

A Latch is level-sensitive: it continuously reads inputs and changes its output as long as the enable signal is HIGH. A Flip-Flop is edge-triggered: it only reads inputs and changes its output precisely at the moment the clock signal transitions (e.g., from LOW to HIGH).
Question 2

What is the "Race-Around Condition" in a simple level-triggered JK Latch?

If $J=1$ and $K=1$ in a level-triggered latch, the output is supposed to toggle. However, if the clock pulse stays HIGH longer than the propagation delay of the gates, the output will toggle continuously and unpredictably ($0 \to 1 \to 0 \to 1$) until the clock goes LOW. Edge-triggering solves this.
Question 3

Based on the Characteristic Equation, what happens exactly when $J=1$ and $K=1$?

Substituting $1$ for $J$ and $1$ for $K$ into $Q_{next} = J\overline{Q} + \overline{K}Q$:

$$Q_{next} = (1)\overline{Q} + (0)Q = \overline{Q}$$
The next state is the exact inverse of the current state. This operation is known as Toggle.
Question 4

Why is the JK Flip-Flop considered the "universal" flip-flop?

It resolves the invalid/undefined state ($S=1, R=1$) found in an SR Flip-Flop by utilizing the Toggle function instead. Furthermore, it can be easily configured to act as a D Flip-Flop (by connecting an inverter between J and K) or a T Flip-Flop (by tying J and K together).
